A 2016 physician survey found that 21 percent of the doctor’s time goes to non-clinical paperwork. That means less time for the patient and more chance of burnout for the doctor.

  Thanks to latest technologies, the vast majority of non-clinical work can be automated, giving physicians more time to do what matters: practice medicine. In fact, according to a recent research report, all healthcare providers will embrace these care delivery-optimization technologies in the next 3-5 years.

All the automated healthcare systems would be linked systematically for the effective data retrieval.
